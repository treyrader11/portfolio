My Portfolio: Learning AngularJS

Below are most of my projects for the Thinkful AngularJS course. Projects focus mainly on an object-oriented architectural approach, JSON, and Angular two-way data binding and routing. All source code is available here.

Click on the images and image titles to read more.


Madlibs App

A word game that dates back to the 1960s and is still around. Users are prompted to supply keywords of their choosing: for instance, a proper name, an adjective, a sport, a dessert item. After making choices, the user plugs each into a preset, paragraph-long text that has blanks which are filled in with the word choices that are made.

A Waitstaff Calculator

This app serves as A tool that helps wait staff at restaurants compute meal costs and track their tips over the course of a shift. I had been provided with a wireframe of the app and was required to write the code from scratch.

A Cajun Music Quiz (refactored)

Applying functions and jQuery to build a "Hot or Cold" game. In this game, the computer randomly selects a number between 1 and 100, and the player then tries to guess the number. The player gets feedback for each guess - "hot" if their guess was close, and "cold" if their guess was far.

AngularJS Ninja App

An app that allows users to add and remove ninjas to and from the DOM. It is sourced from youTube, a tutorial that demostrates Angular routing, data-binding, custom directives, animation, filters, as well as form validation.
